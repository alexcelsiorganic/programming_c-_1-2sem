I. System Requirements

The minimum system requirements:
  • Pentium / AMD K6, 200 MHz
  • Windows 9x / me
  • DirectX version 7.0 or later
  • Graphics card supporting HiColor (16 bit) or TrueColor (24 / 32 bit)
  • Sound card
  • 10 MB of free diskspace
recommended hardware (additional):
  • Pentium II / AMD K6-2, 300 MHz
  • 2D accelerated high performance graphics board (e.g., TNT (2), Voodoo 3, ...)
  • 64 MB memory
  • AMD Athlon C, 1333 MHz :-)

II. Tank Blaster interface

a) The intro

Press any key or click left mouse button to abort the intro.

b) The Startup-menu

Keyboard commands:
 
key command
F1 help
+ increase number of games
- decrease number of games
o display options dialog
esc leave menu and start game

Mouse commands:

Options:
 
bullet reentry:
If this option is active, every bullet leaving the battle field to one of the sides reenters the playing field after "orbiting" the Earth. Possible settings are: (always) off, always on and random. If bullet reentry is active, the small icon shown on the left is visible in the status bar at the bottom of the screen.
number of trees: Determines how many trees will already be existant at the beginning of each level. Available settings: 0 (none), 1, 2 or random (i.e., 0, 1 or 2).
aggressiveness of the cloud: Selects the behavior of the cloud to hitting the cloud with a bullet. Available settings are: 0 (relaxed) - 100 (very aggressive).
quick startup: With this option selected, the presentation of the players and their names at the beginning of each level will be skipped and the battle will start at once.
language: This enables you to change the language of Tank Blaster II. This language menu is also shown at the very first startup of Tank Blaster II.

III. Serious Description Of The Game

a) The game

Blast 'em all and stay alive (if you can). Wipe all enemy tanks off the screen to gain as many credits as possible. But be aware: you are already spotted. On the black market you can get whatever you want to fulfill your task - if you can afford it. Every busted bastard gives you the money for better and bigger (oh yeah) guns, shells, armor. Remember: Saving dollars won't save your life! For the final ranking you'll get 2 points for every blown up tank and 1 extra point per survived level. The nerds toasting themselves pay 3 points every time they drop a bomb onto their feet.

b) The enemy

You Very hard to say. Maybe very dangerous and mean. Likes big weapons.

c) The equipment

Weapons

The store has weapons of two different levels of quality. The "class A" weapons are expensive, but guaranteed to function properly. But everyone, who is short on cash and wants to use big weapons nevertheless, might give the "bargains" a try. In most cases, the bargains are quite cheap, but the store keeper cannot give any guarantee for proper functionality. Besides, bargains are not available all the time...
 
icon description icon description
Tracer. Causes no damage. Just for testing shots without any risk of getting damaged.
Tricerator. Fires three bullets of the type "Incinerator II", each launch angle is varied by some degrees.
Incinerator I. Standard shell, explodes on impact.
Crumbler pI. After getting in contact with the surface, the ground is getting dissolved.
Incinerator II. Heavier explosion than the standard shell.
Crumbler pII. Same as the light version, but much more powerful.
Atomic nuke. Big explosion on impact.
Surprise Egg. They can contain everything, a simple Incinerator I or even a airstrike. The player does not know, what type of bullet he just launched. Rumor has it not being the truth, every 7th egg contains a plasmamelter.
Hydrogen nuke. Huge explosion.
Mosquitos. Once they are released, the mosquitos search for the nearest victim and suck out all his energy. Deadly in most cases.
Plasmamelter. Capable of destroying large areas of the playing field with one detonation.
Shock wave. Has a big scope of action. Unfolds its deadly effect without destroying the surface. Useful if no explosives can be used to avoid water running out.
Security Tree. These trees absorb nearly all bullets. Clever placed, they act as a very effective security barrier.
Airstrike. After placing a signal buoy, the marked area will be blanket-bombed from the air.
Fire ball. Start burning on impact. Main purpose is to burn down nasty trees.
Cluster bomb. After reaching the vertex of the flight path, this bullet bursts into several small bombs.
Water fluctor. Floods the valley next to the point of impact. Water runs out if its borders are destroyed by explosions. This can flood almost the whole battle field.
Laser. Fires a laser beam, strong enough to light even trees.
Moles. Dig themselves in and explode star-shaped below the surface.

Add-ons

icon description
Scroll-o-mat. The scroll-o-mat enables you to have a view at the whole battle field before doing your shot.
Energy shield. Protects your tank from damage of direct or nearby hits or damage from falling down. The shield loses energy every time you are hit.
Tricerator powerup. Powerup for your tricerator. Explosion size level 1: atomic nuke, level 2: hydrogen nuke.
XM-V12 tank. With this mobile tank, you have the opportunity to drive around to a better place instead of doing a shot.
Tank Fuel (F54). The XM-V12 tank requires fuel for driving around.

VI. Trouble Shooting

Possible error messages:

Please make sure, you have installed DirectX version 7.0 or later and that your DirectX is fully functional. This can be verified using the program "DxDiag" (default directory: C:\Program Files\DirectX\Setup) easily.

  • DirectX 'DirectDrawCreateEx'  failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectDraw-function 'DirectDrawCreateEx' produced an error. If you have installed DirectX 7.0 or later, please make sure, no other programs using DirectX are running in the background. Otherwise quit these programs before running Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'SetCooperativeLevel' failed.  DirectX version 7.0 or later required: Tank Blaster II failed getting the exclusive access to your graphics board. This is required to switch the screen resolution. Make sure, no other program using DirectX is running in the background. Otherwise quit this program before launching Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'SetDisplayMode' (640x480, HiColor/TrueColor) failed.  DirectX version 7.0 or later required: Tank Blaster II failed in setting your display to 640x480, 16, 24 or 32 bit per pixel. If your graphics board does not support one of these display modes, you cannot play Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'CreateSurface' failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectDraw-function 'CreateSurface' produced an error. If you have installed DirectX 7.0 or later, please make sure, no other programs using DirectX are running in the background. Otherwise quit these programs before running Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'GetAttachedSurface' failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectDraw-Function 'GetAttachedSurface' produced an error. If you have installed DirectX 7.0 or later, please make sure, no other programs using DirectX are running in the background. Otherwise quit these programs before running Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'CreateClipper' failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectDraw-function 'CreateClipper' produced an error. If you have installed DirectX 7.0 or later, please make sure, no other programs using DirectX are running in the background. Otherwise quit these programs before running Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'DirectSoundCreate' failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectSound-function 'DirectSoundCreate' produced an error. Please make sure, you have installed DirectX version 7.0 or later. Make sure, no other program using your soundcard via DirectX is running in the background. Otherwise quit this program before running Tank Blaster II. If you do not have a soundcard at all, you cannot play Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'SetCooperativeLevel' failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectSound-function 'SetCooperativeLevel' produced an error. Please make sure, you have installed DirectX version 7.0 or later. Make sure, no other program with exclusive access to your soundcard is running in the background. Otherwise quit this program before running Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'CreateSoundBuffer' failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectSound-function 'CreateSoundBuffer' produced an error. If you have installed DirectX 7.0 or later, please make sure, no other programs using DirectX are running in the background. Otherwise quit these programs before running Tank Blaster II.
  • DirectX 'SetFormat' failed. DirectX version 7.0 or later required: The DirectSound-function 'SetFormat' produced an error.  Please make sure, you have installed DirectX version 7.0 or later. Make sure, no other program using your soundcard via DirectX is running in the background. Otherwise quit this program before running Tank Blaster II. If your soundcard does not support the audio format 16 bit stereo, 22 kHz via DirectX, you cannot play Tank Blaster II.
